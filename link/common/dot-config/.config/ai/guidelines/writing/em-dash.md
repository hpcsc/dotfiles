# No Em Dashes

Do not use em dashes. Replace with punctuation appropriate to the role.

## Parenthetical aside or clarification

Use commas.

- Bad: `values from **outside the implementation** — from business requirements — rather than restating`
- Good: `values from **outside the implementation**, from business requirements, rather than restating`

## Introducing a consequence, restatement, or explanation

Use a colon when the second part explains the first. Use a period when both parts are independent thoughts.

- Bad: `acts as a **second opinion** — it can tell you the result is wrong`
- Good: `acts as a **second opinion**: it can tell you the result is wrong`
- Bad: `This can still fail — the formula is a static copy`
- Good: `This can still fail. The formula is a static copy.`

## Labeling list items

Use a colon after the label.

- Bad: `- **Accidental edits** — a merge conflict, a stray keystroke`
- Good: `- **Accidental edits:** a merge conflict, a stray keystroke`
